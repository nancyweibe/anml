Marketing Spotlight

This section celebrates and informs you about our global campaign efforts. Every month you can access a comprehensive overview of a marketing campaign that includes the business opportunity, marketing objectives, the campaign strategy and results.

US Signature Campaign

Business Opportunity

Increase Visa credit volume and revenue among affluent cardholders.

Marketing Objective

Shift competitive card spend to Visa Signature amongst Affluent Visa dual cardholders.

Campaign Strategy

To achieve Visa's business objective of driving Affluent credit growth, our marketing strategy aims to win competitive share by improving our Visa Signature brand awareness and establishing a differentiated positioning that is relevant to the affluent consumer.

Awareness of Visa Signature card drives cardholder engagement with the brand and its benefits. This increased awareness with the brand and assets translates into higher card usage, as aware cardholders spend nearly 30% more than those who are not engaged.

The Campaign Idea:

In order to drive awareness of Visa Signature (VS), we have developed a relevant and differentiated brand positioning in the eyes of our target audience, Affluent dual cardholders who are currently using their VS cards.

Visa Signature's new positioning was developed and tested through extensive primary and third party research. Primary research included ten qualitative consumer groups across the country and quantitative testing of more than 70 positioning statements amongst 500+ affluent dual cardholders.

Initially, the team uncovered three key insights for the Affluent consumer from third party sources including Mendelsohn and Pew Research: (1) Personal relevance over exclusivity; for the affluent consumer "it's about searching for quality and what would work for me"; (2) Experiences; which have a greater perceived value than material things; and (3) Ease, given the hectic and busy life that affluent consumers experience.

Our qualitative research1 suggested the potential for further exploring the territory of personal relevance, as it provided a clear differentiation and a unique opportunity for Visa Signature. Additionally, research highlighted the importance of consumer participation to ensure delivery of personally relevant benefits. Quantitative testing later confirmed personal relevance as a unique and differentiating territory for Visa Signature2.

Anchored in the research, the new Visa Signature positioning of "For those passions in life, Visa Signature brings you the premium benefits that matter to you" was established.

The Campaign Mechanics:
The new campaign, Your idea of what a card should be, launched on April 22, 2013 and runs in-market through August 31, 2013.

Although we have always utilized the voice of the consumer as an input to benefit development, the new campaign brings to life "Your idea of what a card should be" by establishing a new experience on VisaSignature.com.

Consumers' suggestions will be received in our redesigned VisaSignature.com site and will be evaluated by a cross-functional team to assess which ideas could become future benefits. Importantly, this process is designed to moderate idea suggestions by guard railing input topics aligned to the Product Team's long-term asset strategy for Visa Signature.

The campaign leverages today's asset foundation by communicating the core Visa Signature travel benefits of car service, CLEAR and luxury hotel collection as evidence of benefits that are offered in response to cardholder feedback on what matters to them.

Developing this platform required close collaboration with several teams (Asset partners, Cross-agency Team, Product, Sales, VMC and Legal) to help bring this to life. The collaboration will continue with these key members as we evaluate ideas to assess which ones will become future benefits.

Campaign Activation:
The campaign is a fully integrated campaign, with activation across TV, digital, social, mobile, and OOH.

TV provides the foundational reach & scale to establish Visa Signature positioning and branding. Digital, OOH, and social reinforces the positioning by providing a harder hitting offer communication closer to the point of transaction.

Digital:
Creative has been placed in contextually relevant sites to drive awareness and usage intent of relevant benefits closer to the point of transaction. We worked with media partners like Trip Advisor, to explore innovative ways we can seed our messaging when it's most contextually relevant. For our placements on Trip Advisor, we are able to customize our banner messaging, so when users are searching for hotels in cities where we have luxury hotels, we can serve up our ads highlighting the hotels that are in that particular city.

OOH:
Airport OOH are strategically placed to drive awareness of the CLEAR offer. Additionally we'll have placements near airport lounges, to target affluent consumers.
